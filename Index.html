Bioscience Calculus: Animal Growth Dynamics

Universiti Malaysia | SFH: Animal Husbandry Technology

Data Context: Kambing Jamnapari Kacuk

The Bioscience Problem

In Malaysian commercial feedlots, predicting the exact rate of weight gain for crossbred meat goats (like the Jamnapari cross) is crucial for profitability. Feeding animals is expensive. If we keep feeding a goat after its peak growth rate has passed, our feed conversion ratio (FCR) drops, and we lose Ringgit (RM).

By modeling the goat's total weight over time as a function \( f(t) \), we can use calculus—specifically the first derivative \( f'(t) \)—to find the exact rate of change (how many kg the goat is gaining per month) at any specific age.

Reference Data: Modeled using standard somatic growth parameters adapted from MARDI (Malaysian Agricultural Research and Development Institute) guidelines for meat goat production.

Calculus Functions

1. Total Weight Function (Growth Curve)

$$ f(t) = -0.01t^3 + 0.3t^2 + 0.5t + 3.0 $$

Where \( f(t) \) is total weight in kg, and \( t \) is age in months (0 to 12).

2. Rate of Weight Gain (The Derivative)

$$ f'(t) = -0.03t^2 + 0.6t + 0.5 $$

Where \( f'(t) \) represents the growth rate in kg per month. Found using the power rule: \( \frac{d}{dt}(at^n) = n \cdot at^{n-1} \).
